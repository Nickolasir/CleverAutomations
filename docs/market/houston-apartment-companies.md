## Executive Summary

The Houston metro area is one of the largest multifamily markets in the United States, with approximately 700,000+ apartment units across the MSA. The market is characterized by a mix of national institutional owners, Houston-headquartered firms with deep local portfolios, and regional operators. Class A properties are increasingly adopting smart home amenities (smart locks, thermostats, leak sensors) as a differentiation and rent premium strategy.

CleverBuilding targets property management companies with 500+ units in Houston, offering building-wide smart infrastructure with per-unit isolation, centralized management, and a resident-facing app. This document profiles the top 20+ companies by Houston unit count, their current technology adoption, and decision-maker targets.

## Current Smart Home Technology Adoption in Houston Multifamily

| Technology | Adoption Rate (est.) | Common Vendors | Typical Deployment |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Smart Locks** | 35–40% of Class A | Latch, Yale, Schlage, August | Unit entry + common areas |
| **Smart Thermostats** | 25–30% of Class A | Ecobee, Nest, Honeywell | In-unit energy management |
| **Leak Sensors** | 15–20% of Class A | SmartRent, Meld | Under sinks, water heaters |
| **Package Lockers** | 60–70% of Class A | Luxer One, Parcel Pending, Amazon Hub | Lobby/mail room |
| **EV Charging** | 15–25% of Class A | ChargePoint, Tesla, EverCharge | Garage/parking |
| **Smart Lighting (common area)** | 20–25% | Lutron, Philips | Lobby, hallways, amenity spaces |
| **Resident App** | 40–50% of Class A | SmartRent, Zego, Entrata | Rent payment, maintenance requests |
| **Access Control (common area)** | 50–60% of Class A | ButterflyMX, Latch, LiftMaster | Gate, garage, building entry |
| **Full Smart Home Platform** | 5–8% of Class A | SmartRent (dominant) | Lock + thermostat + leak sensor suite |

### Key Insight

Only 5–8% of Houston Class A apartments have deployed a full smart home platform (typically SmartRent). This means **92–95% of the market is either untouched or piecemeal** — a massive addressable opportunity for CleverBuilding's integrated platform.

## SmartRent: The Incumbent to Displace

SmartRent (NYSE: SMRT) is the dominant enterprise smart apartment platform and the primary competitor in this vertical.

| Attribute | SmartRent | CleverBuilding (Ours) |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Units Deployed** | 700,000+ nationwide | 0 (pre-launch) |
| **Hardware Cost/Unit** | $300–500 installed | Target: $150–250 installed |
| **Monthly SaaS/Unit** | $5–10/unit/mo | Target: $3–7/unit/mo |
| **Hardware** | Proprietary hub + sensors | Raspberry Pi-based Clever Hub |
| **Lock Integration** | Yale, Schlage, Kwikset | Any Zigbee/Z-Wave/Thread lock |
| **Voice Assistant** | Amazon Alexa built-in | Custom voice (sub-1s, privacy-first) |
| **Resident App** | Yes (SmartRent app) | Yes (CleverBuilding resident app) |
| **Maintenance Integration** | Yes (work orders) | Yes (proactive maintenance alerts) |
| **Privacy** | Cloud-dependent, Amazon ecosystem | Local processing, cloud-optional |
| **Open Protocol** | Limited (proprietary) | Matter, Zigbee, Z-Wave, Thread |
| **Multi-tenant Isolation** | Yes | Yes (cryptographic unit isolation) |

**Our Angle**: 50% lower hardware cost, privacy-first local processing, no vendor lock-in, voice assistant that does not listen for Amazon/Google, open protocol support for any device.

## Sales Approach by Company Size

### Enterprise (10,000+ Houston units): Greystar, Camden, Highmark

- **Approach**: Formal RFP/RFI response; executive briefing; multi-stakeholder selling
- **Timeline**: 6–12 months
- **Pilot**: 1 property (200–300 units)
- **Decision-makers**: VP/SVP level + procurement
- **Key metric**: Total cost of ownership vs. SmartRent; NOI impact

### Mid-Market (3,000–10,000 Houston units): Morgan, Venterra, Nitya, Finger, Hanover

- **Approach**: Direct outreach to C-suite or VP; dinner meeting; model unit demo
- **Timeline**: 3–6 months
- **Pilot**: 1 property (100–200 units)
- **Decision-makers**: President/CEO or VP of Operations
- **Key metric**: Rent premium, resident satisfaction, maintenance reduction

### Emerging (500–3,000 Houston units): Veritas, Senterra, Judwin, Memorial Mgmt

- **Approach**: Direct to owner; lunch meeting; property tour + install demo
- **Timeline**: 1–3 months
- **Pilot**: 50 units at one property
- **Decision-makers**: Owner/Principal
- **Key metric**: Differentiation from competing properties in same submarket
